About

I've loved working with software and hardware for a long time. I remember my dad coming home one day with a Radioshack bag in his hand, filled with a dizzying array of wires, lightbulbs, batteries, and switches. I spent the rest of the night trying to get this one damn lightbulb to turn on when I turned on a switch. Just like the hardware my dad bought, I believe that computer science is not just about writing code for the sake of writing code. Rather, it's a tool you use to solve problems, or to turn a damn lightbulb on.

You'll find me working on everything from operating systems to cloud computing, combining my understanding of low level and data driven development derived from my projects. For the past couple years, I've had the opportunity to lead software engineering and converse with education leaders at Youth Coding Workshops, working to bring programming literacy to local middle schools.
